# Where to find datasets (and how to vet them)

> **License warning up front:** open the Hugging Face dataset card and confirm the
> license *before* you train anything you'll ship. Two silent traps: (1) `cc-by-nc-*`
> = **non-commercial** (research only), and (2) data **synthetically generated by a
> closed model** (GPT-4, Claude, Gemini) whose terms may forbid training a competing
> model. Both are common in popular instruction/function-calling sets. IDs and
> licenses below were accurate at authoring time but drift â€” always re-check the card.

## Searching the Hugging Face Hub effectively

Most public data lives at huggingface.co/datasets. Don't trust a pretty card â€”
inspect the actual rows:

1. **Filter by task + modality** (Text Generation, Question Answering) and **sort by
   Trending / Most downloads / Recently updated**. Downloads + likes are a decent
   proxy for "battle-tested."
2. **Open the Dataset Viewer** and read 10â€“20 real rows. Check: is it already in
   `messages` form? ShareGPT `conversations`? flat instruction/response columns?
   This decides how you invoke `prepare_fireworks_dataset.py`.
3. **Read the "Data Fields" / config** for splits and column names.
4. **Check size** (rows and bytes) against the ~1000+ SFT floor and the 1 GB upload
   ceiling.
5. **Search by intent, not just keyword**: e.g. "function calling", "tool use",
   "agent trajectories", "code instruction", "chain of thought", "<your domain>".
6. Programmatic search: `huggingface_hub.HfApi().list_datasets(search=..., sort="downloads")`.

### Domain-specific
Search the Hub for your domain (`medical`, `legal`, `finance`, `support`). Domain
sets are smaller and messier â€” expect to filter hard and often to combine with the
user's own data. A few clean thousands beat a noisy hundred-thousand.

## When no dataset fits: use your own data, or synthesize

- **Your own data is the gold standard.** Support tickets, PR reviews, chat logs,
  docs Q&A â€” anything showing the exact inputâ†’output you want. Even a few hundred
  real examples often beats a big generic set. Map them to `messages` with the
  converter (flat-column or ShareGPT mode).
- **Synthetic distillation.** When you have none: prompt a strong teacher model to
  generate inputâ†’output pairs *in your target format*, review/filter them, then
  train K2.7 on the result. Watch the teacher model's terms of use, and always
  human-spot-check â€” models faithfully learn synthetic mistakes.

## Quality checklist before you commit to a dataset

- [ ] **License** verified on the card and compatible with the user's use.
- [ ] **Fit**: rows actually demonstrate the Step-1 target behavior (filter the rest).
- [ ] **Format-able**: you know the source shape (messages / conversations / columns).
- [ ] **Dedup**: near-duplicate rows removed (the converter's `--dedup` helps).
- [ ] **Decontaminate**: remove any overlap with a benchmark you'll report on.
- [ ] **Volume**: â‰¥~1000 usable examples for SFT (else augment or go RFT).
- [ ] **Length**: examples fit the training context (drop/`--max-context` the rest).
- [ ] **Spot-check correctness**: read 10â€“20 assistant outputs; bad targets are
      learned faithfully.
